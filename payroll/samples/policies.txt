**Salary & Attendance Rules** for the institution:

### **1. Attendance & Salary Calculation**
*   **Daily Rate Determination**: The monthly basic salary is divided by **25 working days** to calculate the "Per Day Salary"[cite: 1].
*   **Standard Deduction Unit**: A "Full Day’s Salary" refers to this calculated per-day amount[cite: 1].

### **2. The 3-Day Late Threshold (General Policy)**
*   **0 – 2 Late Days**: No salary deduction occurs if the total number of late arrivals is two or fewer (provided each delay is 20 minutes or less)[cite: 1].
*   **3 Late Days**: Upon the 3rd late arrival of the month, a deduction of **one full day's salary** is automatically applied[cite: 1].

### **3. The 20-Minute Rule (Immediate Penalty)**
*   **Late > 20 Minutes**: If a teacher is late by more than 20 minutes, a **300 Taka fine** applies immediately starting from the very first day it occurs[cite: 1].
*   **Overlap**: This fine is independent of the 3-day threshold; it is triggered by the duration of the delay[cite: 1].

### **4. The 4th Day+ Fine Scale (For Delays ≤ 20 Mins)**
Once a teacher reaches their **4th late day** (and subsequent late days) in a single month, additional fines are applied based on the duration of the delay:
*   **1 – 5 minutes late**: 100 Taka fine[cite: 1].
*   **6 – 10 minutes late**: 150 Taka fine[cite: 1].
*   **11 – 20 minutes late**: 200 Taka fine[cite: 1].

### **5. Absence and Leave**
*   **Approved Casual Leave (CL)**: No deduction is made for absences marked as authorized Casual Leave[cite: 1].
*   **Unpaid Absence**: Any absence not covered by an approved CL results in the deduction of the **Per Day Salary** for each day missed[cite: 1].
